Important Disclaimer

Labour law rates (minimum wages, PT slabs, LWF amounts) are revised by state governments every 6–12 months. Central acts are amended by Parliament. The framework in this guide is accurate as of mid-2025 — but specific numbers must always be verified from official government portals before acting on them.

📋 How to Verify — Step by Step
1
Central Acts (EPF, ESI, TDS) — Go directly to labour.gov.in or epfindia.gov.in. Check the latest circulars section. EPFO issues wage ceiling and contribution rate notifications via official circulars.
2
State Minimum Wages — Visit your state labour department portal. Look for "Minimum Wages Notification" — typically a PDF gazette notification. These are revised every April 1 or October 1.
3
Professional Tax Slabs — Search your state's finance department or labour department for "Professional Tax Rates [Year]". PT is amended via state Finance Acts, usually in the state budget (Feb–March annually).
4
Cross-check with a CA or CS — For anything involving penalties, filings, or registrations — always get sign-off from a Chartered Accountant (CA) or Company Secretary (CS). They subscribe to tax update services and will flag recent amendments.
5
Use Shram Suvidha for returns — shramsuvidha.gov.in consolidates returns for 6 central labour acts. Filing here is treated as compliance under those acts. The portal itself reflects current requirements.
6
Subscribe to official gazettes — Both Central (egazette.nic.in) and state gazettes publish all amendments. Set a Google Alert for "[Your State] labour law notification" to catch changes as they happen.
